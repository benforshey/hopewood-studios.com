Hopewood Music Studios began in 1993 as a home-school family of music teachers. We offer private instruction in at several locations and classroom instruction at one location, when there are students of similar playing levels whose schedules will allow group classes to be arranged.

The teachers of Hopewood Music Studios utilize the Suzuki method in teaching piano, cello, guitar, and voice in private lessons and group classes. Music theory, ear-training, and sight-reading are also included in the instruction.

Samuel Howell is a lifelong Suzuki student and teacher with a decade of teaching experience. He has been privileged to study with a number of exceptional artists and educators including Chris Nunnally (ECU, Juilliard) Sharon Lawrence (Hampton Sidney) John Kaboff (Kaboff cello school, Arlington, VA) Dr Aurelius Gori (NOVA, Gori Voice Studios, Kensington MD) Dr Mark Whitmire (NOVA; St James’s, Richmond, VA) and Pamela Devenport (Suzuki Association of the Americas).

Sam takes a holistic approach to music education. He understands music to be intrinsically physical and social as well as artistic. His pedagogical approach places a strong emphasis on healthy, relaxed physical technique applying principles of the Feldenkrais method. His greatest aspiration as an educator is to inspire the artistic awareness in the community through comprehensive musical education and performances that are both illuminating and accessible to local audiences. Sam holds an Associates of Applied Arts in Vocal Performance from Northern Virginia Community College and is a member of the Suzuki Association of the Americas.

Toni Howell traces her love of music and theater to the influences of growing up in a culturally sensitive area, between New York City and Philadelphia, where there was a strong emphasis on the arts. She received her B.A. in Communications and Theater Arts from Susquehanna University in Pennsylvania where she studied voice, specifically opera, with James Wilhelm (City Opera of New York and New York Metropolitan Opera).

Toni has directed opera, as well as acted in and directed many plays, sang lead musical comedy roles in summer theater, and spent a short period of time with Lamb’s Players of San Diego. She taught all subjects (except math) to 7th and 8th graders and music to K through 8. She has been teaching voice and piano for 20 years, most recently taking the training and becoming registered as a Suzuki teacher. She founded and directed the Storysingers, a music club in Halifax County, as well as directed various choirs and ensembles.

Toni home-schooled 5 children, now grown, who are all excellent musicians, all Suzuki-trained. She practices Suzuki's methods in teaching children and adults because she has found that her students progress more rapidly with it and gain a solid, disciplined technique, but she also recognizes the value of the traditional method with its emphasis on reading and incorporates that emphasis in her program as well. She teaches all styles of singing, but personally loves singing musical comedy, opera, and classic folk. Disciplined performance is a major goal; truth in communicating is another.

Suzuki Method

The Suzuki method, also called Talent Education, was developed by Dr. Shinichi Suzuki in the first half of the twentieth century. Dr. Suzuki believed that all children in the world show great natural capacities by speaking and understanding their mother languages. He sought to combine "a music teaching method with a philosophy that embraced the total development of the child."

The success of our method is dependent on the reinforcement of the lesson by the parent at home. Therefore, we request that a parent attend every lesson with the young child. Adult students are also welcome. Dr. Suzuki's method works for grown-up's, too!

Pricing and Policy

There are 15 lessons per session: Winter (January–April), Spring (May–August), and Fall (September–December).

I offer three payment options: by the sesson ($300—equals $20 / lesson), by the month ($80—equals $24 / lesson), and by the lesson ($30 / lesson).

  •   No refunds will be given.
  •   Lessons at your home may be an option, depending on location and schedule. Transportation costs will be added to tuition fee.
  •   Students taking lessons at the Prizery in South Boston will be charged 10% extra, due to the rental fee on the studio.
  •   Lessons are generally 30 minutes long, though longer lessons can be arranged for advanced students.
  •   Payment is due at the first lesson of each semester.
  •   When it is necessary for the student to miss a lesson, make-up lessons will be offered during the scheduled breaks, or at other off-times, but only two lessons per semester will be made up. If the teacher misses a lesson, it will be made up or a refund given.
  •   Scholarships based on need are available for serious students.
  •   Parents are expected to attend the lessons of younger students.
